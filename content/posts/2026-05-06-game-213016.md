The recent remarks by Alix Wilton Regan, the actress set to portray Lara Croft in the upcoming Tomb Raider games, highlight a crucial conversation in the gaming industry: the recognition of video game actors for their performances. Regan passionately argues that these performers, who often endure demanding conditions on performance capture stages, deserve acknowledgment for their contributions to the gaming experience.

Regan's call for recognition is echoed by her admiration for the cast of Baldur's Gate 3, who have inspired many in the industry to appreciate the value of their work. As the gaming landscape evolves, it becomes increasingly important to celebrate the artistry behind video game performances, including motion capture and voice acting. With upcoming titles like Tomb Raider: Legacy of Atlantis and Tomb Raider: Catalyst on the horizon, the spotlight on these actors is more relevant than ever.

As we look forward to the future of gaming, will the industry finally give video game actors the recognition they deserve? This is a pivotal moment for performers, and the conversation is just beginning.
