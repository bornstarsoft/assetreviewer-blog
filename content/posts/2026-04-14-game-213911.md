Nintendo has marked the 25th anniversary of its beloved Animal Crossing series with a delightful surprise for players of New Horizons. To celebrate this milestone, the company has rolled out update 3.0.2, which not only fixes various bugs but also gifts players a commemorative Leaf Statue, reminiscent of the original Dbutsu no Mori game that first graced the Nintendo 64 in 2001.

In addition to the in-game gift, Nintendo has also released new music for its Nintendo Music app, featuring the soundtrack from the original GameCube version of Animal Crossing. This nostalgic touch not only honors the franchise's rich history but also enhances the experience for both new and veteran players alike.

As fans eagerly embrace these updates, its exciting to think about what other surprises Nintendo might have in store for the future of Animal Crossing. Will we see more content updates or perhaps even new titles? Only time will tell!
