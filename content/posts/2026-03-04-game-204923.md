The Game Design and Implementation program at Capital Region BOCES stands out as a vital educational initiative, equipping students with essential skills for the booming gaming and tech industries. Over nearly a decade, this program has successfully bridged the gap between education and industry demands, allowing students to engage in hands-on projects that foster creativity, collaboration, and technical proficiency.

Under the guidance of instructor Astra Wijaya, students like Michael Satterlee and Mikkail Warner are diving deep into the world of game development. They are not just learning to code; they are transforming traditional board games into immersive virtual experiences. This practical approach not only enhances their coding skills but also prepares them for diverse career paths in software engineering, 3D printing, and cybersecurity.
