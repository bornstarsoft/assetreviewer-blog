The gaming world is buzzing with concern as Nagoshi Studio, founded by Yakuza series creator Toshihiro Nagoshi, faces potential closure due to funding cuts from publisher NetEase. Just three months after the announcement of their debut title, Gang of Dragon, the studio has reportedly been informed that it will lose crucial financial support, which could jeopardize the game's completion.

NetEase's decision to withdraw funding stems from the revelation that an additional 7 billion (approximately $44.4 million) is required to finish Gang of Dragon. Despite Nagoshi's efforts to secure alternative funding, there has been no success so far. This situation reflects a broader trend, as NetEase has been scaling back its international game development operations after years of aggressive investment.

As fans eagerly await the release of Gang of Dragon, the uncertainty surrounding Nagoshi Studio raises questions about the future of innovative gaming experiences. Will Nagoshi be able to rally support to continue this promising project, or is this the end of a new chapter in gaming?
