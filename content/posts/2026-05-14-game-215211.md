The gaming world is buzzing with the recent launch of Phase 2 for "Neverness to Everness," which went live on May 13, 2026. Despite the ongoing AI controversy that has cast a shadow over the game's global debut, Hotta Studio is pushing forward with exciting new content. This update introduces a limited S-class character, Hotori, and a fresh arc banner, alongside a variety of events that will engage players until June 3.

Hotori is the star of the Misty Tipsy Style Limited Board, a unique gacha mechanic that allows players to roll dice and collect rewards. The player-friendly design ensures that if you don't pull Hotori within 70 rolls, the odds improve, and a guaranteed pull is available by the 90th roll. This approach not only enhances player experience but also builds anticipation for future banners, as pity mechanics carry over.

As we look ahead, the drip marketing for Version 1.1 has already teased two new characters, Lacrimosa and Chaos, keeping the community engaged and eager for what's next. Will Hotta Studio manage to navigate the challenges posed by the AI controversy while continuing to innovate and captivate its audience? Only time will tell.
